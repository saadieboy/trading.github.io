Who can carry out activities on the Forex market in the Russian Federation?
Only professional participants in the securities market who have a corresponding license from the Bank of Russia (Federal Law No. 39-FZ dated April 04, 1996 No. 39-FZ "On the Securities Market") can carry out transactions on the Forex market with individuals who are not individual entrepreneurs. the current wording, in particular Section II and Article 4.1). The Bank of Russia imposes significant requirements on such participants, including mandatory membership in self-regulating organizations and requirements for equity capital of a forex dealer.

Why does Alpha Forex set a leverage of no more than 1:50?
The ratio of the amount of the client’s security (pledge) and the amount of its obligations cannot be less than one to fifty (1:50) (clause 9 of Article 4.1. Of the Federal Law No. 39-FZ of April 4, 1996 “On the Securities Market”) . In practice, this means that the leverage can be any, but with an increase in the leverage and obligations of the client, the forex dealer will be forced to raise the stop out level, upon reaching which the position is forced to close. For example, if a forex dealer sets a leverage of 1: 100, then the stop out level will be 200%. For the leverage we chose, we considered it reasonable to set the stop out level at 85%.

What is a spread?
Spread is the difference between the purchase price (ask) and the sale price (bid) of a financial instrument that is traded on the market at any given time. It is due to this difference that banks, brokers and dealers make a profit on the purchase / sale operations carried out by their customers. The value of the spread depends on a number of parameters, such as volatility and liquidity in the market. If the minimum value of the spread bank, broker or dealer can keep at a constant level under calm conditions in the market, then during the growth of volatility and / or a drop in liquidity, the maximum value of the spread will be determined by the market participants themselves, thus the spread increases dramatically.

What is slippage?
Slippage means the possibility of executing an order of the “Stop” type at a price worse than that specified in the order. The probability of price slippage is a consequence of the principle of order execution. Limit orders are an order to buy at a lower price (that is, better) than at the current moment, or sell, respectively, at a higher price (that is, also better) than at the current moment.

Unlike “Limit” orders, the prices indicated in “Stop” orders at the time of their placement with the Forex dealer are worse than the prices available on the market at the time of placing the “Stop” order, i.e. in the “Stop” order for purchase, the price is indicated higher than the price currently on the market, and in the “Stop” order for sale the price is lower than the price currently available in the market.

Therefore, unlike the “Limit” order, which contains the desire to buy or sell at a price better than what is now on the market, and which in the literal sense are bids for buying or selling (therefore, they are executed at their own price), the “Stop »Contain a completely different desire - to buy or sell some asset in the market when the market rate reaches the price specified in the order.

Thus, the “Limit” (take profit, buy limit, sell limit) and “Stop” orders (stop loss, buy stop, sell stop, stop out) orders are completely different in their market sense and order execution order, which Unfortunately, it is not fully understood by some traders who believe that the “Stop” or “Limit” orders are just different names.

Obviously, “Stop” orders cannot immediately “go into the market”, since the price indicated in the order is worse than the current price on the market, and such an order would be immediately executed, since there are always willing to sell you more than now on the market or buy from you cheaper than now on the market. For this reason, the “Stop” order starts being executed when the market reaches the price specified in it, and is actually executed at the first price available to the forex dealer.

What is Gap?
"Gap" - the occurrence of a price gap in the market, when the course can move up or down discretely, without making deals at intermediate prices, since they were not in the market. Price gaps often occur, for example, at the time of opening of the market on the night from Sunday to Monday, when important events for the market occurred on weekends. Accordingly, the activation of orders during the formation of a gap occurs at the first market prices, which were formed after the “gap”.

How to calculate margin on locked deals?
Margin (security for an open position) is always taken in the base currency - the currency in the pair in the designation in the first place. After that, it is converted into the currency in which the trading account is nominated.

The calculation takes place in four stages.

1. Calculation of the weighted average position opening price.

Formula: Pср = (Open Price 1 × Lot 1 + Open Price 2 × Lot 2 + ... + Open Price X × Lot X) / (Lot 1 + Lot 2 + ... + Lot X), where:
- Pср - weighted average price
- Open Price 1 — opening price of the first position;
- Open Price 2 — the opening price of the second position;
- Open Price X — the price for opening a position X;
- Lot 1, 2,...Х    — volume of position X in lots.

2. Calculation of margin by locked (hedged) volume.

Formula: M1 = (Pср × Lots х 100 000) / Leverage / 2, where:
- Pср - weighted average price, calculated in item 1;
- Lots - cumulative volume of locked positions in lots;
- Leverage - leverage.

3. Calculation of the margin on the remaining (non-localized) volume.

Formula: M2 = (Pср × Lots х 100 000) / Leverage, where:
- Pср - weighted average price, calculated in item 1;
- Lots - cumulative volume by non-locked positions in lots;
- Leverage - leverage

4. The calculation of the final margin.

Formula: Margin = M1 + M2

Example.Calculation of margin for multiple transactions for currency pairs.

Trading account dollar, leverage - 1:40

Data on three transactions:

- sale of 0.5 lot USDRUR at 66.0227;
- purchase of 0.8 lot USDRUR at 67.0312;
- sale of 1.4 lots USDRUR at 66.7011.

Calculation of the weighted average price:

Pcp = (66.0227 x 0.5 + 67.0312 x 0.8 + 66.7011 x 1.4) / (0.5 + 0.8 + 1.4) = 66.6733 USDRUB

Margin calculation by locked (hedged) volume. The volume of locked positions 0.8 × 2 = 1.6 lots.

M1 = (66.6733 x 1.6 x 100,000) / 40/2 = 133 346.60 RUB

 Calculation of the margin on the remaining (non-localized) volume.

M2 = ((2.7-1.6) x 66.6733 x 100 000) / 40 = 183 351.56 RUB The

calculation of the total margin.

Margin = 133 346.60 + 183 351.56 = 316 698.16 RUR
